Book of Commandments Chapter 2 (1833)

Compared with Doctrine and Covenants Section 3 (2013)

Change 1 (verse 10): Verse changed to past tense at the end, and the statement of the future result of Joseph Smith's repentance is replaced with a statement clarifying the disobedience. This is the first of many examples of Joseph Smith editing the future and present-tense text to sound present or past-tense respectively.

Change 2 (verses 16-18): Instead of a knowledge of the Nephites coming to the Lamanites, it is the knowledge of the Savior that is coming to the remnant of both the Nephites and the Lamanites through the testimony of their fathers (the righteous Nephites). I suspect there are two reasons for this shift. 1) To focus on the testimony of Christ instead of the people, and 2) to make clear that the remnant of the Nephites will also receive the Book of Mormon and learn about their righteous ancestors in addition to the Lamanites receiving the record.

1 A Revelation given to Joseph, in Harmony, Pennsylvania, July, 1828, after Martin had lost the Manuscript of the forepart of the book of Mormon, translated from the book of Lehi, which was abridged by the hand of Mormon, saying:

Revelation given to Joseph Smith the Prophet, at Harmony, Pennsylvania, July 1828, relating to the loss of 116 pages of manuscript translated from the first part of the Book of Mormon, which was called the book of Lehi. The Prophet had reluctantly allowed these pages to pass from his custody to that of Martin Harris, who had served for a brief period as scribe in the translation of the Book of Mormon. The revelation was given through the Urim and Thummim.

THE works, and the designs, and the purposes of God, can not be frustrated, neither can they come to nought,

1 The works, and the designs, and the purposes of God cannot be frustrated, neither can they come to naught.

for God doth not walk in crooked paths; neither doth he turn to the right hand nor to the left; neither doth he vary from that which he hath said: Therefore his paths are strait and his course is one eternal round.

2 For God doth not walk in crooked paths, neither doth he turn to the right hand nor to the left, neither doth he vary from that which he hath said, therefore his paths are straight, and his course is one eternal round.

2 Remember, remember, that it is not the work of God that is frustrated, but the work of men:

3 Remember, remember that it is not the work of God that is frustrated, but the work of men;

for although a man may have many revelations, and have power to do many mighty works, yet, if he boasts in his own strength, and sets at nought the counsels of God, and follows after the dictates of his own will, and carnal desires, he must fall and incur the vengeance of a just God upon him.

4 For although a man may have many revelations, and have power to do many mighty works, yet if he boasts in his own strength, and sets at naught the counsels of God, and follows after the dictates of his own will and carnal desires, he must fall and incur the vengeance of a just God upon him.

3 Behold, you have been intrusted with these things, but how strict were your commandments; and remember, also, the promises which were made to you, if you did not transgress them;

5 Behold, you have been entrusted with these things, but how strict were your commandments; and remember also the promises which were made to you, if you did not transgress them.

and behold, how oft you have transgressed the commandments and the laws of God, and have gone on in the persuasions of men:

6 And behold, how oft you have transgressed the commandments and the laws of God, and have gone on in the persuasions of men.

for behold, you should not have feared man more than God, although men set at naught the counsels of God, and despise his words,

7 For, behold, you should not have feared man more than God. Although men set at naught the counsels of God, and despise his words—

yet you should have been faithful and he would have extended his arm, and supported you against all the fiery darts of the adversary; and he would have been with you in every time of trouble.

8 Yet you should have been faithful; and he would have extended his arm and supported you against all the fiery darts of the adversary; and he would have been with you in every time of trouble.

4 Behold thou art Joseph, and thou wast chosen to do the work of the Lord, but because of transgression, if thou art not aware thou wilt fall,

9 Behold, thou art Joseph, and thou wast chosen to do the work of the Lord, but because of transgression, if thou art not aware thou wilt fall.

but remember God is merciful: Therefore, repent of that which thou hast done, and he will only cause thee to be afflicted for a season, and thou art still chosen, and wilt again be called to the work;

10 But remember, God is merciful; therefore, repent of that which thou hast done which is contrary to the commandment which I gave you, and thou art still chosen, and art again called to the work;

and except thou do this, thou shalt be delivered up and become as other men, and have no more gift.

11 Except thou do this, thou shalt be delivered up and become as other men, and have no more gift.

5 And when thou deliveredst up that which God had given thee sight and power to translate, thou deliveredst up that which was sacred, into the hands of a wicked man,

12 And when thou deliveredst up that which God had given thee sight and power to translate, thou deliveredst up that which was sacred into the hands of a wicked man,

who has set at nought the counsels of God, and has broken the most sacred promises, which were made before God, and has depended upon his own judgment, and boasted in his own wisdom,

13 Who has set at naught the counsels of God, and has broken the most sacred promises which were made before God, and has depended upon his own judgment and boasted in his own wisdom.

and this is the reason that thou hast lost thy privileges for a season,

14 And this is the reason that thou hast lost thy privileges for a season—

for thou hast suffered the counsel of thy director to be trampled upon from the beginning.

15 For thou hast suffered the counsel of thy director to be trampled upon from the beginning.

6 Nevertheless, my work shall go forth and accomplish my purposes, for as the knowledge of a Savior has come into the world, even so shall the knowledge of my people,

16 Nevertheless, my work shall go forth, for inasmuch as the knowledge of a Savior has come unto the world, through the testimony of the Jews, even so shall the knowledge of a Savior come unto my people—

the Nephites, and the Jacobites, and the Josephites, and the Zoramites,

17 And to the Nephites, and the Jacobites, and the Josephites, and the Zoramites, through the testimony of their fathers—

come to the knowledge of the Lamanites, and the Lemuelites and the Ishmaelites, which dwindled in unbelief, because of the iniquities of their fathers, who have been suffered to destroy their brethren, because of their iniquities, and their abominations:

18 And this testimony shall come to the knowledge of the Lamanites, and the Lemuelites, and the Ishmaelites, who dwindled in unbelief because of the iniquity of their fathers, whom the Lord has suffered to destroy their brethren the Nephites, because of their iniquities and their abominations.

and for this very purpose are these plates preserved which contain these records, that the promises of the Lord might be fulfilled, which he made to his people;

and that the Lamanites might come to the knowledge of their fathers, and that they might know the promises of the Lord, and that they may believe the gospel and rely upon the merits of Jesus Christ, and be glorified through faith in his name; and that through their repentance they might be saved: Amen.
