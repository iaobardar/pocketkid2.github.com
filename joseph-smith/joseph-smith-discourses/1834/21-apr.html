Discourse of Joseph Smith given April 21st, 1834

2 Manuscript sources, 2+ Printed editions

Event: Conference of Elders

Location: Brother Carpenter's house, Norton, Ohio

This discourse of Joseph Smith was given at the opening of a conference. The themes contained in it revolve around the importance of Zion and modern revelation, and being prepared for the second coming. The clerk of the conference was Oliver Cowdery, and Orson Hyde sometime soon after copied the minutes into Minute Book 1. They were then lightly edited and copied into the Manuscript History, around June 1843, by Willard Richards. The two primary printed sources are the Times and Seasons and final volume set of the History of the Church, but it should be noted that the discourse also appeared in Teachings of the Prophet Joseph Smith.
