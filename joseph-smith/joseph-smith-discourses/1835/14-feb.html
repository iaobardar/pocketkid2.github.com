Discourse of Joseph Smith given February 14th, 1835

2 Manuscript sources, 2 Printed editions

Event: Conference of the church

Location: Kirtland, Ohio

This discourse was given at a special conference where Joseph Smith spoke about Zion's Camp and blessed those who had participated. It was at this conference that the first members of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles were selected by the three witnesses to the Book of Mormon, and confirmed by the First Presidency.

Kirtland February 14 1835

This day a meeting was called of those who journeyed to Zion for the <purpose of laying> the foundation of its redemption last season with as many more of the Brethren & Sisters as felt disposed to attend. President Joseph Smith Junr presided over the meeting. After the Congregation assembled. he arose and requested the attention and read the 15th chapter of John and said, let us endeavor to solemnize our minds that we may receive a blessing by calling on the Lord & said let us pray

After an appropriate and affecting prayer was made the Bretheren who went to Zion, were requested to take their seats together in one part of the house by themselves.
